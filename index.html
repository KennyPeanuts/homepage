introduction

teaching

I am currently a professor at Longwood University where I teach a variety of courses on ecology, environmental science, and biogeochemisty.

Through my courses I hope to help students acquire the skills they need to support a life-long struggle to understand the natural world. My courses do not provide a body of facts to be interpreted as immutable cannon but rather strive to support students with the tools they need investigate the material on their own.

You can learn more about the courses I teach or get access to my teaching materials on my teaching page

research

My lab's research investigates the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem functioning. Our primary focus is the factors that control the decomposition of organic matter in lakes and ponds.

For information about our specific research projects or if you are a student interested in joining the lab, please visit my research page.
